What is SIP?

SIP is a sales incentive plan. Sales incentive plans are intended to reward sellers for their performance and results that align to the overall success of the organization. SIP plans support our customer growth strategy, promote customer focus and reward profitable sales.

Sales incentive plan design / redesign is a multi-step process that includes planning, designing, and rolling out new plans. It also involves monitoring the plans throughout the year and analyzing results to ensure that the plans are working effectively.

Best Practice Guidelines

Component Best Practice Guidelines
Plan Structure Appropriate number of distinct or unique plans for available sales roles
Eligibility

Eligibility is determined at the job and not incumbent level

Primary job function must meet ALL three of the following criteria:

  • Significant customer / partner contact and relationship
  • Persuade the customer to purchase a product/service
  • Clear and quantifiable sales objectives
Pay Levels
  • Set Target Total Compensation at the appropriate percentile to attract and retain the right talent
  • Benchmark to the right roles based upon job content; use internal equity when no market benchmark exists
  • Benchmark to select group of companies based on where the firm competes for product and talent. When needed, expand the definition to include companies with similar sales motions
Pay Mix
  • Pay mix reflects the job’s degree of individual customer or business partner persuasion while considering corporate pay philosophy and market benchmarks
  • Jobs with the most individual persuasion have more aggressive pay mix (more at-risk)
  • Use a global pay mix structure that fits each role’s degree of risk tolerance
Performance Measures and Weights

Measure rules:

  • < 3 measures to keep focus (1-2 measures preferred)
  • Weights reflect the job’s focus and have impact >=20% of Target Incentive (TI)

Criteria include:

  • Alignment to strategy and job
  • Influence their measures
  • (Line of Sight)
  • Ability to set quotas/goals
  • Ability to track
  • Objective metrics

Use Key Sales Objectives to drive business outcomes that cannot be tracked with sales (requires governance investment)
Mechanics and Pay Curves

Simple mechanics:

  • Quota-based incentive mechanic to accommodate varied territories/quotas
  • Commission rate when roles have equal territories/quotas

Pay curves link pay with performance:

  • Use thresholds to limit pay for recurring revenus
  • Use leverage and excellence levels to set accelerator rates
  • Provide unlimited upside, unless extreme payouts occus
    • Use linkages (gates, multiplier, modifiers, and matrices) to link measures together if necessary
    • Use uplifts and downlifts to drive strategic focus if necessary
    • Use windfall policy, decelerator rates, deal caps, or pay caps to manage extreme payouts
Performance & Payout Frequency

Performance periods should:

  • Match sales cycle length
  • Set realistic goals
  • Align to annual budget whenever possible
  • Minimize pushing/pulling orders

Payment frequency should:

  • Occur close to sales event
  • Ensure proper cash flow
  • Be consequential

Appropriate calculation methodology based on:

  • Quarterly vs. annual focus
  • Ability to push/pull orders
  • Advanced accelerated payouts
Quotas / Targets
  • Quotas are fair, realistic, stretch goals based on sales potential
  • Motivational participation rate (50-60% of incumbents achieve goal)
  • Consistent quota methodology for similar roles/segments
  • Well documented and communicated
  • Process includes both top-down and bottom-up inputs
Crediting and Policies

Sales crediting rules:

  • Align to strategy
  • Pay for persuasion
  • Minimizes financial risk
  • Recognize quota setting difficulties
  • Can be efficiently administered
  • Validated source (no self-reported)
  • No budget inflation

Policies for:

  • Management rights
  • Employee changes (e.g. new hires, terminations, transfers, leaves of absence)
  • Quota changes
  • Windfall deals/mega deals
  • Sales crediting
Special Incentives & Rewards

SPIFFs/Contests will:

  • Not compete with annual sales compensation plan
  • Can not exceed 25% of recipient’s target incentive
  • Not exceed 5% of population’s TTC
  • < 5 special incentives a year per job
  • Last no longer than 1-3 months
  • Communicate as % of target incentive to accommodate varied pay levels

Recognition programs to acknowledge top performers and reinforce company values

Definitions of Key SIP Terms

Total Target Cash (TTC)

Base Salary + Target Incentive

Target Incentive (TI)

Portion of TTC that is not guaranteed – Paid upon meeting goals

Target Pay Mix

Split between Salary and Target Incentive; expressed as % of TTC (e.g., 60/40)

Leverage

Target Incentive earned by top performers (commonly benchmarked to 90th percentile); typically expressed as a multiple of Target Incentive (e.g., 3x)

Performance Measures

Metrics against which incentive pay is awarded (e.g., revenue, profitability, key sales objectives)

Threshold

Minimum performance to earn incentive pay

Hurdle

Ties upside payouts to the outcomes of a secondary performance measure

Excellence

A design point where top 10% of sellers earn full upside

Cap

Maximum amount of earnings available - Not a preferred mechanic

Decelerator

A design point where the payout rate decreases; typically applied at excellence or excluded from plan design
