What Type of Data is Needed?

Accurate and current data collection is a crucial part of the SIP process. Without clean and comprehensive data, cost modeling may result in errors and negatively effect individuals.
  • Data will help inform design and drive decision making. Data is needed at the incumbent level for the individuals impacted.
  • Data is typically extracted from CRM systems like Salesforce.com and HRIS systems.
  • Data should be provided in Excel and must include employee ID.
Data Category Data Details
Latest Sales Roster
  • Demographic data (Name, Tenure, ID, geography)
  • Job Data (position title, job mapping)
Pay Data by Incumbent
  • Base Salary
  • Variable target (target incentive or expected earnings)
Performance Data by Incumbent
  • Goal / quota by measure
  • Performance against goal / quota by measure
  • Total volume performance (if commission based)
Variable Payout by Incumbent
  • Variable pay per measure

Data Analysis

Analyzing the current state SIP helps establish a baseline and determine what is working well and what are areas of opportunity and improvement.

Assessing the incumbent SIP plan involves qualitative and quantitative feedback regarding the effectiveness of the program

Qualitative assessment methodologies:

  • Web-based survey to get overall feedback on how the plan is working
  • One-on-one Interviews with key stakeholders such as sales managers and individual sellers

Quantitative assessment methodologies:

  • Analyzing total variable compensation payout versus budget
  • Analyzing annual quota performance per incumbent
  • Analyzing the correlation between payout and performance per measure
  • Analyzing variable payout per incumbent

Key Questions that Data Analysis Will Answer

  1. Do the current plans fall in line with our budgets?
  2. Are our top performers currently earning a proportional amount to their performance?
  3. Are our team members meeting their margin and revenue goals?
  4. Is there a good differentiation of payout between low and high performers?
  5. Is our salesforce motivated by the current plan designs?
