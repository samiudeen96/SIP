Implementation Strategy Best Practices

Effective Change Management principles are required to successfully implement and communicate even the best-designed sales compensation programs.
  • Absolute buy-in from the executive team
  • A compelling reason (business case) for change
  • Support from relevant stakeholders and influencers in the organization
  • Cascading communication approach; no one is left out of the loop
  • Appropriate level of training and communication collateral for all employees
  • A smooth transition strategy for all individuals to the new plan
  • Administrative systems and reporting must be developed and functioning when the new program goes live
  • Communicate a theme, not just mechanics
  • Use leadership messages
  • Develop presentations and scripts
  • Train sales managers
  • Provide web-based documentation and calculation models
  • Give ongoing access to pay and performance results
  • Show how pay and performance contributes to company and personnel rewards

Roll Out Timing Best Practices

Best practices to ensure a successful rollout:
  • Obtain plan approval in time to update all administration systems in order to provide timely first period payouts
  • Communications must include strategic goals and how plan links to those goals
  • Obtain design team and legal approval on all communication materials before rolling out new plan
  • Sales leadership must play a role in communicating the plan, generally kicking it off with how it links to the strategy. Another resource can go through the details if needed
  • Cascade communications to ensure all relevant parties are aware of the program before their direct reports
    • Sales leaders who were not involved in design process – generally prior to new fiscal year
    • Sales management – generally prior to new fiscal year, but may be at beginning of fiscal year
    • Individual contributors – generally at beginning of the fiscal year so as to not disrupt year-end activities
    • Note: try to sync communications with other sales events, like kick-off meeting
    • Best to bring similar roles together in one meeting/call so everyone hears the same message as opposed to each manager running their own roll out
  • Try to link communications of plan design with quota whenever possible
  • All managers should have a 1x1 meeting with their direct reports to review new quota and plan details and discuss how employee can achieve/over-achieve their quota their quota and make money on their new plan

Who Should Communicate the Plan Changes?

Effective communication starts with the right channels.
Sales Leadership
  • Sales Leadership should own the sales compensation plan.
  • The sales compensation plan is the Sales Leader’s biggest lever to drive strategy and profitable revenue growth
  • Sales Leadership needs to publicly own the pay program; communicating the new plan is a means of demonstrating leadership
Sales Operations
  • Sales Ops typically provide enabling solutions to the salesforce like CRM
  • Using Sales Ops to communicate new plans implies the plan is just an administrative program which reduces motivational impact.
Human Resources
  • HR is not in a position to defend or enforce the new pay program
  • HR can assist with preparation of the communication material
  • HR should answer questions related to HR practices or employment related policies
Finance
  • Finance has an important oversight role with the salesforce
  • If finance communicates the plan, sales personnel will harbor suspicions regarding crediting, goals and earning opportunities

Change Management Communication

Key elements for all Executive Messages:
  1. Address / Acknowledge Current State
    • “Over the last year we embarked on an initiative to better align our compensation with our overall strategy.”
  2. Disconnect from the Past
    • “We are looking forward to a bright future.”
  3. Vision of the Future
    • “With these new plans, we expect to better retain the talent we need as well as ensure that we appropriately reward for success.”
  4. How we’ll get there
    • “Over the next week, you will be seeing detailed information from your Sales Manager, including plan estimators to clearly understand how you stand to benefit.”
  5. Call to Action
    • “I’m looking forward to embarking with you on the journey to an exciting year to come!”

Executive Communications Memo Example

Leadership memo example template to be delivered to the entire salesforce.

FROM: Sales Leader

SUBJECT: Announcement: Compensation Program Changes

As we look to the future, we regularly review our sales programs to ensure that our go-to-market strategy continues to support our customer experience objectives and optimize our revenue opportunities in a continually evolving marketplace. Over the last year we embarked an initiative to better align our compensation with our overall strategy.

We are looking forward to a bright future and sustained growth. As such, with these new plans, we expect to better retain the talent we need as well as ensure that we appropriately reward for success. We are confident that many of you will be pleased with the changes to the new compensation plans. Additionally, we have designed the plans to better reward above target performance.

Over the next week, you will be seeing detailed information from your Sales Manager, including plan estimators to clearly understand how you stand to benefit. We’re looking forward to rewarding you for accelerated growth as we move forward in our new go-to-market strategy. I’m looking forward embarking with you on the journey to an exciting year to come!

Communication and Implementation Pitfalls to Avoid

Communication Plan Pitfalls to Avoid Train-the-Trainer Pitfalls to Avoid Cascading Communication Pitfalls to Avoid Post-Launch Communication Pitfalls to Avoid
  • Lack of clear communication dates
  • Long gaps between communications
  • Not sharing calendar with leadership team
  • Unclear meeting objectives and agendas
  • Not scheduling enough time to cover all the details
  • Jumping to plan details (skipping strategic messaging)
  • One-way communication vs. an interactive session
  • Absence or neglect of lower priority plans
  • Not allowing for feedback or Q&A
  • Not establishing clear expectations and next steps
  • Communicating plans too early
  • Lack of collateral to support the conversation
  • Elongating the communication process
  • Losing sight and/or control of the message
  • Inconsistent messaging from managers
  • Limiting clarification to only those that ask questions
  • Lacking a formal feedback process or loop
  • Not leveraging feedback to inform future plan designs

Collateral Materials to Ensure a Successful Rollout

Collateral that clearly explains how the new plans work, the rationale for changes and how to maximize earnings needs to be distributed to the incumbents impacted.
  1. Comprehensive plan document
    • Include key sections such as eligibility, general terms and conditions, compensation elements, crediting rules, quotas and definitions of key terms
  2. Variable incentive calculators
    • Simple excel-based tool that allows sellers to calculate their incentive earning based on their performance
  3. Payout examples
    • Payout examples that clearly illustrate how each measure and mechanic works
  4. Examples of “how to win”
    • Payout examples that illustrate how to maximize earnings

Plan Document: Design Details

Plan documents should be clear and objective.

Key Sections:

  • Plan Overview
  • How to be Successful on the Plan
  • Plan Components
  • Measure Details – definition, calculation methodology, rates and pay curve (visual if possible)
  • Calculation Example
It is important to note that this document is confidential and should only be shared with the appropriate party(ies)

Plan Document: Plan Acknowledgement Form

Plan acknowledgement forms should contain the following:

Key Sections:

  • Employee and Total Pay Details
  • Target Incentive by Measure Details
  • Quota (and ICR) Details
  • Acceptance Language
  • Signatures

Plan Document: Policy Details

Ensure plan policies are clear and easily accessed.

Key Sections:

  • Eligibility
  • General Terms and Conditions
  • Compensation Elements
  • Crediting Rules
  • Employee Changes

  • Compensation Review Board
  • SPIFF
