Plan

  1. Alignment of business strategy, GTM approach, sales coverage and organization design decisions
  2. Positions defined consistently with clear accountability and behaviors

Assess

  1. Collect sales force feedback
  2. Analyze pay-for-performance, effectiveness
  3. Compare plan results to appropriate market data (pay and practices)
  4. Ongoing sales performance and operations reporting is performed

Design

  1. Align with compensation design principles
  2. Clear line of sight between measures and outcomes
  3. Measures are feasible
  4. Plans are competitive with external market
  5. Design includes cross functional input

Administer

  1. Systems track and report on progress, metrics
  2. Mid-year crediting or sales target adjustments reviewed through governance
  3. Employee results communication and payment processing

Implement

  1. Plans, targets and territories determined prior to new fiscal year
  2. Legal reviews
  3. Administration system setup
  4. Change and Communications Management
  5. Governance

Expected SIP Timeline (High-Level)

The SIP Process should be initiated in Q1 and be finalized before the end of the fiscal year to ensure successful rollout for the upcoming fiscal year.

Typically, implementation teams are composed of similar members as the design team, but the ownership and roles shift.
MD (recommended)

Owns overall compensation plan and outcome; communicates strategy

Sales Management

Helps communicate plans and drive performance

Sales Operations

Administers performance and payout

Human Resources

Generates communication content and drives change management

Project Manager

Owns project execution, clarifies objectives, oversees day-to-day operations

Other

Inputs from IT, Marketing and Finance

Note: Whereas Total Rewards will often play a very strong role in organizing plan design activities, business resources like those listed above will take over primary accountability for planning and driving plan implementation activities.
