Since quotas and headcount plans can impact cost estimates, clients may confuse cost modeling with an exercise in quota and headcount planning. Cost modeling does not automatically include these two topics in scope and should be executed accordingly.

Cost Modeling Decision Guidance

Depending on the plan designs and historical data availability, there are various ways to develop a cost model.
Plan Design Is Historical Performance Data Available? Cost Model Guidance
Commission Yes Apply the commission rate to historical performance to project payout
No Simulate volume performance by using a random number generator and apply commission rate to simulated performance
Quota-Based Bonus Yes
  • Apply the proposed payout curve to the historical quota attainment performance
  • Using the historical quotas, simulate quota attainment performance using a random number generator for low, mid and high scenarios to forecast a range of payouts
No Simulate quota attainment performance by using a random number generator and apply proposed payout curve to the simulated performance

The Customized Approach to Cost Modeling

Typically, at least 3 performance scenarios are used during cost modeling in order to show the range of pay at various levels of organization performance:

Low Performance: Majority of incumbents below target quota attainment

Target Performance: Ideal performance distribution, with a mean of 100%

High Performance: Majority of incumbents above target quota attainment

The critical activity and assumption behind the modeling scenarios is what to use for your distribution (not the mean) – actual historical data can be used, or distributions can be simulated with assumptions using Excel tools (whether based on historical data or not).

Cost Modeling Process Steps

Assess 1 Collect required data and information during data collection phase
Align 2 Go through alignment phase and compensation design meetings
Design 3 Document new plan design parameters (or options)
4 Confirm final design proposal with team
5 Architect the model and confirm the approach with your team
6 Build the model in Excel based on your approved architecture
7 Iterate, run sensitivity analyses, and finalize designs using the model
8 Confirm final design with team
9 Build final design summary and costing report

Data Collection for Cost Modeling

Process Guidance
  • All information required for cost modelling should be covered under the typical data request for a compensation plan
  • This information should be requested prior to or at the very beginning of a project
Employee and To-Be-Hired (TBH) Data

Employee-level data for in-scope roles, including pay and bonus information and plan assignments

can also include to be hired information if desired

  • Employee Data
    • Name, Title, Location, Team
    • Plan Assignment
    • Target Total Comp
    • Pay Mix
    • Base Salary
    • Target Incentive
  • TBHs
    • # by Title, Location, Team
    • Start Date
    • Prorated Average Target Total Comp
    • Pay Mix
    • Prorated Average Base Salary
    • Prorated Average Target Incentive
Incumbent Performance History

From a cost modeling perspective, performance history should be collected so that accurate performance distributed assumptions can be made in the case of new products or roles, historical performance is not relevant.

  • Prior Period Quotas / Goals (may modify)
  • Prior Period Performance (may modify)
  • Next Period Quotas (if available)
  • Historical pay and performance by measure at the incumbent level
Current Plan Designs

Complete plan design details should be collected for both current plans and future designs. While only future may be needed to meet project needs, current is typically collected as well and may be needed to create plan comparison analyses.

  • Pay Mix
  • Measures and Weights
  • Mechanics and Linkages (Hurdles, Matrixes, Etc.)
  • Pay Curves
    • Commission Rates
    • Thresholds
    • Excellence Points
    • Leverage
    • Inflection Points
    • Caps/Decelerators
  • Performance Periods
  • In-Plan Uplifts (Sales Credit and/or Rate/Payment Uplifts) and Add-On Bonuses

Every Cost Modeling Report Should Include Three Components

Executive Summary

Highlight the key results of the modeling and call out important areas for confirmation or alignment.

Cost Results

Chart that present the results of the model across each scenario. A table with the detailed results by role or measure is often included underneath the chart.

Approach & Assumptions Summary

Document and present key approach and assumption information to share with the client.
