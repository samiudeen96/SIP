Expected SIP Timeline (High-Level)

The SIP Process should be initiated in Q1 and be finalized before the end of the fiscal year to ensure successful rollout for the upcoming fiscal year.

SIP Process Overview-Assessment

SIP should be monitored on an ongoing basis to ensure that it is working effectively, and a formal assessment should be conducting annually.
  • Plan performance should be collected and assessed on an ongoing basis
  • Plan participant feedback should be collected through a survey or informal discussion
    • Feedback should be analyzed and shared across the team
  • Plan effectiveness analyses should be conducted
    • Analyses examples include pay-for-performance analysis and quota performance distributions

SIP Assessment Process

Assessing the SIP program helps determine what is working well and there are areas of opportunity and improvement.
  • Assessing the SIP plan involves qualitative and quantitative feedback regarding the effectiveness of the program
  • Qualitative assessment methodologies:
    • Web-based survey to get overall feedback on how the plan is working
    • One-on-one Interviews with key stakeholders such as sales managers and individual sellers
  • Quantitative assessment methodologies:
    • Analyzing annual quota distributions per measure
    • Analyzing the correlation between payout and performance per measure
    • Analyzing variable payout distribution

Qualitative Survey Question Examples

Before redesigning the SIP program, it is important to understand where opportunities for improvement exist. An effective way to do this is by conducting a survey for SIP eligible incumbents. Below are sample Likert-style survey questions:
“On a Scale of 1 to 5 with 1 being Strongly Disagree, and 5 being Strongly Agree, how much do you agree with the following statements?”
1 I believe that my target total compensation/on-target earnings (base + target incentive) is competitive with target total compensation/on-target earnings offered by other companies in my industry.
2 I believe that top performers are paid competitively compared to top performers at other companies in my industry.
3 I am comfortable with the amount of pay in my target incentive versus my base salary pay.
4 I feel that the measures in my plan align with my organization’s sales strategy.
5 I have the ability to affect my measure(s).
6 I understand how I receive sales credit for compensation purposes.
7 I understand how my incentive pay is calculated.
8 My incentive plan motivates me to over-achieve my assigned goals.
9 My management clearly communicated my plan to me.
10 The plan document clearly communicates the plan details so that it is easy to understand.
11 I can easily track my sales performance.
12 I can easily track when my incentives are earned.
13 I believe my payments are accurate and timely.
14 Open-question for other feedback

One-on-one Interview Question Examples

Interviewing key stakeholders helps to understand the sales strategy and compensation feedback. It is then possible to compare interview findings to identify perceptional mismatches.
1.
Describe your compensation plan.
2.
What is the biggest positive about the current compensation plans?
3.
Does your compensation plan align with business goals to which you are assigned?
4.
Who is responsible for communicating regarding your compensation plan? Is that communication effective?
5.
How do you identify your top performing salespeople and account mangers?
6.
Describe the quota-setting process.
7.
What input do you provide in the quota-setting process?
8.
Does the current sales incentive compensation program motivate to meet or exceed your goals?
9.
What would you like to see improved in your current compensation plan?
10.
What haven’t we spoken about during this call that you wanted to make sure we heard?
11.
If you could summarize the top takeaway regarding your SIP, what would it be?

How to Interpret Pay-for-Performance Analysis

What to look for in a Pay for Performance Chart:
  • Ideal Curve
  • Higher performers receive higher incentive pay
  • Few outliers
  • There is a strong correlation (high R2)
  • Weaker correlation between performance and pay
  • Minimal reward for over performance
  • Poor differentiation in pay for top and bottom performers
  • (1) High variability in pay for same level of over performance
  • (2) Same pay for different performance

How to Interpret Quota Distribution Analysis

This analysis is conducted if the team has a goal-based plan. A Quota distribution should typically resemble a bell-shaped curve, with 50%-60% of team members exceeding quota.
