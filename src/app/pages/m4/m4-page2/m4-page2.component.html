Measure and Weights Decisions

Measures should align to the specific behaviors the role is designed to perform.
Measures & Weights

Measures are the activities/results by which salespeople are assessed and paid

Measures should be designed with consideration to:

  • ✔
    Alignment to strategy and job
  • ✔
    Influence how time is spent
  • ✔
    Line of Sight
  • ✔
    Ability to set targets/goals
  • ✔
    Ability to track
  • ✔
    Objective metrics

Measures are assigned weights, which represent the amount of target incentive pay assigned to a measure

  • Weights should reflect the job’s focus on that particular activity

No more than three measures; no less than 20% weighting each

How to Determine Measurement Scope

  • Where possible, jobs should participate in only one type of plan: individual, team, or group
  • Plans that use multiple measure levels should ensure proper balance between the components (weighted appropriately and linked if necessary)
  • Avoid using team or group measures for jobs where there is no unifying leadership or shared mission
  • Individual Contributor
    • Measured and rewarded at the individual level
  • Account Manager or Territory Rep
  • Work Team
    • Measured and rewarded on collaborative team efforts
  • National Account Team
  • Group (Common Workforce)
    • Measured and rewarded at system-wide level
  • Frontline Customer Care Team

Pay Curve Decisions

Pay curves should be strategically designed to maximize the potential of the sales compensation system.
Mechanics & Pay Curve

Pay curves explain how salespeople earn via illustrating the percentage of target incentive earned at various percentages of quota achieved

Individual element decisions – e.g., threshold, leverage, cap, etc. – work to construct pay curves for each job

Typically, compensation plans should:

  • Set thresholds at point of recurring business
  • Use quotas to accommodate varied territory
  • Use excellence to set accelerator rates
  • Decelerate rate after excellence to avoid over payment

Mechanic Above Excellence Decisions

Excellence and beyond, pay curves may continue to accelerate, decelerate to a less lucrative rate, or cap payments.
Continued Acceleration Deceleration Capped
Payout rate continues to accelerate past excellence point Payout rate decelerates upon reaching excellence point Payout is capped upon reaching excellence point

Use Case:

  • Provides continued motivation

Use Case:

  • Cost Savings
  • Difficult to set quotas
  • Mitigates risk from “Blue Birds”

Use Case:

  • Higher cost savings
  • Difficult to set quotas
  • Protection from “Blue Birds”
  • Little influence after certain level of performance
