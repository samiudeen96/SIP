Eligibility

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Significant customer and/or partner contact and relationship
  • Influence on the customer to generate revenue for Cargill
  • High level of sales process involvement/ownership
  • Clear and quantifiable sales objectives

Target Pay Mix Decision

Percentage of pay-at-risk should align to amount of persuasion involved in the job role.
Pay Mix
  • Pay mix is the percentage of target total compensation (TTC) derived from base salary compared to pay-at-risk
  • Designed by job, with minimal variance across individuals in the same job role
  • Reflects the job’s degree of individual customer persuasion while considering corporate pay philosophy and market benchmarks
  • Jobs with the most individual persuasion have more aggressive pay mix (more at-risk)

Leverage Designs

Like pay mix, a plan’s leverage should align to amount of persuasion involved in the job role.
Leverage
  • Leverage is the percentage of target incentive earned by top performers, referred to as a plan’s Excellence point
  • Generally, top performers are defined as the 10% of achievers for a measure
  • Leverage is the percentage of target incentive those top performers earn
  • Communicated as a fixed ratio of the upside opportunity to target incentive
  • Leverage should be aligned to the degree of influence involved in a sales role
  • Jobs with the most influence on the purchasing decision have a higher upside earning

Leverage and Pay Mix Design in Practice

Target pay mix informs how payouts should be structured, however, actual pay mix at fiscal year end may differ based on plan design and performance. This slide and the next will show examples of how these can affect the actual pay mix.

Because this seller achieved 50% of their annual quota, the total target incentive payout was $7,500. This results in an actual pay mix of 86% in base salary and 14% in incentive, with the total payout being $87,000.

Because this seller achieved 175% of their annual quota, the total target incentive payout was $71,875. This results in an actual pay mix of 51% in base salary and 49% in incentive, with the total payout being $146,875.
