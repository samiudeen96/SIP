Pay & Performance Periods Decisions

Performance periods depend on length of sales cycle and ability to accurately set quotas.
Pay & Performance Periods

Performance periods should:

  • ✔
    Match sales cycle length
  • ✔
    Set realistic goals
  • ✔
    Align to annual budget whenever possible
  • ✔
    Minimize pushing/pulling orders

Payment should:

  • ✔
    Occur close to sales event
  • ✔
    Ensure proper cash flow
  • ✔
    Be Consequential
Discrete
Performance is measured and paid out for each discrete period, without consideration for past performance.
Cumulative
Performance and pay are measured cumulatively against longer period.
Quarterly with Annual True-Up
Performance is measured and paid each quarter (capped at 100% or reduced rate) with full acceleration paid at year end.

SPIFF Guidelines – Definitions and Facts

What are SPIFFs, and how should they be administered?
Topic Description
SPIFF Definition
  • Short-term, complementary contests providing focus and additional earnings opportunities for eligible sales positions. Most often, SPF programs focus on a specific product initiative/roll-out, expanding a specific market/customer group or prioritizing a certain sales target within a district or territory. Special incentive programs should NOT compete with any business objective or sales compensation program.
Eligibility
  • Eligibility must be tied to specific positions able to directly impact desired plan outcomes through the chosen SPF plan measures. Sales management should not participate through a roll-up of team results.
Timing
  • Duration of each SPIFF should typically be no longer than three months. There should be no more than four special incentives in a fiscal year and only one contest run at a time.
Cost
  • Annual budget for special incentive programs should typically be 5% or less of the eligible population’s Total Target Cash (TTC).
Funding
  • A financial analysis must be completed to ensure the contest can be funded via business outcomes being driven by the SPF program. The analysis should show the total estimated cost of the contest (how much will be paid out to the field at target) as well as the additional revenues anticipated to be generated. As a general guideline, the ratio of payout costs to additional sales generated should typically be 5% or less.
Payouts
  • Target and maximum award amounts possible through all SPF programs planned within a fiscal year should be determined so that payout opportunities (in cash or cash equivalents) do not compete with key sales priorities included as a part of the employee’s primary sales incentive plan.
Measures
  • There is not one standard for determining measures and targets that will apply to each SPF plan. When establishing measures and targets, businesses and Groups should choose drivers supporting the achievement of approved strategic goals.
  • Team based measures are typically avoided.
  • When possible, special incentive programs should leverage sales output metrics as opposed to activity metrics.
Plan Features

The following program features are considered less effective and should be avoided:

  • “Winner-take-all” programs
  • Random chance winners
  • Predictable annual programs
  • Performance objectives already addressed in the compensation plan.
